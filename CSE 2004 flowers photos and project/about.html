Project Motivation

The Language of Flowers is one of my favorite novels, and I have always been drawn to the way the main character, Victoria, communicates her emotions to the people she loves through flowers. It was captivating to me that she could convey such complex feelings through symbols without saying a word. That idea became the starting point for this project.

I wanted to adapt this form of communication to the web and place it within a historical lens through floriography, the Victorian language of flowers. By combining the emotional world of the novel with floral definitions, historical reference, and interactive design, this project explores how symbols and structured visual systems can communicate meaning in intuitive and expressive ways.

Book Inspiration

Narrative Source

The Language of Flowers

Author: Vanessa Diffenbaugh

The Language of Flowers is a contemporary novel that reintroduces readers to the Victorian tradition of floriography through the story of a young woman named Victoria who uses flowers to express emotions she struggles to communicate directly.

Throughout the book, different plants symbolize specific feelings, and bouquets become a subtle language for expressing love, grief, forgiveness, and hope.

The novel demonstrates how symbolic systems can carry emotional depth and personal meaning. It inspired the idea that flowers can act as a form of emotional vocabulary, where individuals construct messages through combinations of symbols.

Reference Source

Floriography

Author: Jessica Roux

Floriography: An Illustrated Guide to the Victorian Language of Flowers is an illustrated botanical reference that catalogues dozens of flowers and their symbolic meanings from Victorian-era floral dictionaries.

Each flower entry includes its traditional meaning, historical context, and botanical illustration. The book presents floriography as both a visual and linguistic system.

Its structured format - combining imagery, taxonomy, and symbolic meaning - influenced the visual language of this website. The botanical plate style, centered compositions, and minimal layouts used in this project draw heavily from the design approach of Roux's work.
