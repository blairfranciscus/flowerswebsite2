Colorado Facts

Each card lists an important fact about Colorado! Hover over the fact to see an effect!

Statehood

Colorado became the 38th state on August 1, 1876, which is why it is called the Centennial State.

Highest Peak

Mount Elbert reaches 14,440 feet above sea level, making it the highest summit in Colorado.

State Flower

The Rocky Mountain columbine became Colorado’s state flower in 1899, so the foreground blooms echo a real emblem.

Four Parks

Colorado has four national parks: Rocky Mountain, Great Sand Dunes, Mesa Verde, and Black Canyon of the Gunnison.

Divide Line

The Continental Divide crosses Colorado, splitting water flow toward the Pacific basin or the Atlantic basin.

Fourteeners

Colorado is famous for its fourteeners, the peaks that rise above 14,000 feet and define so much of the state’s mountain culture.
