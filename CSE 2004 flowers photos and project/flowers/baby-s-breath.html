Baby's Breath

Gypsophila

Victoria's Definition: Everlasting love

Meaning
  • Purity
  • Innocence
Origin
In the late nineteenth century, the Gypsophila plant was dubbed "baby's breath," due to its lovely scent and small, delicate blooms. Similar in appearance to the intricate lace of a bridal veil, this flower is frequently used in wedding bouquets as well as in arrangements for new mothers.
Pair With
  • Lily as a gift for new parents
  • Queen Anne's lace as a gift for a godparent, to thank them for protecting and caring for the child
