Marigold

Tagetes
Meaning
Grief
Origin
When clouds roll in or night falls, the marigold curls inward and lets its head droop. When it opens again in the sunlight, its petals, wet with dew, appear to be crying. Traditionally, marigolds are used to celebrate Dia de los Muertos (Day of the Dead) in Mexico, when the spirits of the departed are believed to visit the living. This celebration is rooted in the Aztec festival honoring Mictecacihuatl, the goddess of the underworld.
Pair With
  • Willow to indicate sorrow at the loss of a loved one
  • Rue to apologize for the pain you've caused
